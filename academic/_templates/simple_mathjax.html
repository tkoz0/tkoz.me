Algebra

Linear Equations

A linear equation can be written in slope-intercept form as \(y=mx+b\) or in point-slope form as \(y-y_0=m(x-x_0)\).

Linear equations can be plotted by choosing 2 points and drawing a straight line connecting them.

Quadratic Equations

A quadratic equation has the form: \(ax^2+bx+c=0\) where \(a,b,c\in\R\) and \(a\neq0\). The solution(s) is(are) real when \(b^2-4ac\geq0\) and is:

\[ x=\qf{a}{b}{c} \]

Below is a table of values for the quadratic function \(y=f(x)=x^2\):

\(0\) \(0\)
\(1\) \(1\)
\(2\) \(4\)
